Criminal Victimization in the United States -- Statistical Tables

Cathy T. Maston

May 12, 2011    

Presents 110 tables with detailed data on major variables measured by the National Crime Victimization Survey (NCVS).

Download:
One table at a time | Complete set of tables | Sections of tables

Topics covered include —

  • crimes of violence (rape, sexual assault, robbery, assault) and theft (pocket picking, purse snatching, burglary, theft, and motor vehicle theft), with data on victim characteristics (sex, age, race, ethnicity, marital status, income, and residence)  
  • crime characteristics (time and place of occurrence, distance from home, weapon use, self-protection, injury, medical care, economic loss, and time lost from work)
  • victim-offender relationship
  • victims' perceptions of alcohol and substance use by offenders and of offender characteristics (age, race, and sex)
  • whether crimes were reported to the police and reasons why
  • police response time for reported crimes

These statistical tables are available only in electronic formats. A list of all reports in this series that were printed is provided on the publications page.

Download one table at a time, all the tables, or a section of the tables:

One table at a time
Table index - Keyword access to the most current table through drill down index. Spreadsheets and tables from prior editions are also available.

Note: Due to changes in methodology, the 2006 National Crime Victimization rates are not comparable to previous years and cannot be used for yearly trend comparisons. However, the overall patterns of victimization at the national level can be examined. See Criminal Victimization, 2006.
