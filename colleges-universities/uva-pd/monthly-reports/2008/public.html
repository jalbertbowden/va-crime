Public Notices

These notices provide the public with information on criminal incidents on or around U.Va. grounds. By informing the public, we hope new information, possible leads, and/ or witnesses may be produced which will enable us to solve a case. Special precautions and press releases may also be listed. If you have any information about a incident listed, please contact U.Va. Police or Crime Stoppers (434) 977-4000.

  1. $55,000 REWARD SEXUAL ASSAULT SUSPECT

    2. The Charlottesville, Albemarle County, and University of Virginia Police Departments are requesting the continued assistance of the public in the identification of the black male individual who is responsible for at least seven (7) sexual assaults within the metropolitan area of Charlottesville, Va. Genetic testing has linked this series of sexual assaults with the most recent attack taking place on 08/18/2004 in Albemarle County.

    The joint task force that has been vigorously investigating these cases would like to once again stress the importance of the public's safety awareness. All residential doors, windows, etc., should remain locked at all times and the public should remain alert and focused on their surroundings, both at home and away.

    The Task Force offers the following observations regarding this offender:

    Black male between the ages of 20 and 40 years of age.

    A range in height from 5'6" to 6'.

    A range in weight from 150 to 220 pounds.

    A medium to muscular athletic-type build.

    Eyes that stand out as being extremely white.

    Comfortable in and around the Charlottesville area.

    The Task Force strongly believes that individuals in the community may have important information related to these crimes. Anyone with information regarding these incidents are encouraged to contact Task Force personnel through one of the below listed means:

    Serial Sexual Assault Hotline 1-866-405-2519
    Crimestoppers - 434-977-4000
    Email: wanted@albemarle.org

    Written correspondence can be mailed to Detective Randy Higgins P.O. Box 494 Charlottesville, VA 22902

  2. On April 24, 2004 an unidentified suspect removed a screen from a window on the West Lawn. The suspect then attempted to enter through the window when the resident was awakened, confronting the suspect. The suspect fled the area in an unknown direction.

    3. The suspect is described as a white male. He is approximately 20 years old. The unidentified suspect is approximately 6’ tall. At the time of the incident he was wearing a white ball cap and an orange or yellow t-shirt.

    After responding to the above mentioned Breaking and Entering, University Police canvassed the Lawn area for similar incidents. Police found a second room where the screen was removed but no entry into the room was gained.

  3. In May 2004, University Police charged a University of Virginia student with making a bomb threat. The student was charged with Virginia Code section 18.2-83 which is a class 5 felony.

  4. Assault- REWARD! $22,000: (Posted 3-14-03)- On February 26, 2003 at about 1:56 a.m. a U.Va. student was assaulted on Poe Alley located near "The Lawn" on our campus. The suspect was described as a heavy set white male, wearing a dark jacket, light pants, and a dark baseball cap. This reward is offered to anyone who has information that leads to an arrest and conviction of the offender in this incident.
