The Town of Big Stone Gap recognizes that protecting the lives of our citizens and their property is a basic service of Town Government. The Police Department, Fire Department, and the Rescue Squad each share in the responsibility of realizing this common goal. The members of these organizations are well trained, hard working, and are always a phone call away. The volunteer members of these organizations donated in excess of 6,000 hours of community service during 1997.

 
Police

The Big Stone Gap Police Department works aggressively to make the Town of Big Stone Gap a safe community for its citizens and visitors. The following is a brief synopsis and highlights of activities of the Department for the year 1997. The Department is comprised of::
 

Chief of Police 
1 Sergeant 
3 Detectives 
2 Lieutenants 
7 Full Time Sworn Officers 
1 Secretary 
2 Records Clerks 

 
 

1997 Highlights

Calls answered 2,960 
Assault Cases 60
Crimes Against Persons & Business 144
Crimes Against Property 36 
Value of Stolen Property $91,284.00 
Value of Stolen Property Recovered $30,553.00 
Patrol miles driven 90,337 
Animal Control Complaints 1,987 
DUI Arrests 68
Accident Reports Worked 168
Traffic Violations 429
Courtesy Citations 140 

 

The Big Stone Gap Police Department is involved in several community activities which include:

DARE - Drug Abuse Resistance Education
Neighborhood Watch Program
Law Enforcement Torch Run for Special Olympics
Lonesome Pine Hospital Health Fair
DSI - Developmental Services Handicap Program

 Fire Department  
   

The Big Stone Gap Volunteer Fire Dept. is a local volunteer organization dedicated to the protection of life and property within the Department's service area and to neighboring communities when needed.  The Big Stone Gap Volunteer Fire Dept. is comprised of:
 

Members

1 Part Time Chief 
26  Volunteer Members 

Locations

Station #1 Shawnee Avenue 
Station #2 Big Stone Heights 

 

 Equipment  

1988 Boardman Pumper 
1969 Dodge Mini Pumper 
1974 Ford Pumper 
1979 Dodge Pumper 
1985 Chevy Equip. Truck
1982 Chevy Mini Pumper

   

 1997 Highlights  

On 11-18-97, the Town Council approved bids for purchase of two new fire trucks. 

The Fire Department answered 54 inside calls, and 52 outside calls, a total of 106 fire calls. 

In excess of 1,386 hours were expended by the volunteers through practices and fire suppression. 

The average response time to an alarm is consistently between 2 - 4 minutes. 

  The Big Stone Gap Volunteer Fire Department is involved in several community activities which include:

Fire education programs for pre-school programs, and classes between K-6
Fire Evacuation and Education programs for businesses
Assisting in Town parades.

Rescue Squad  

The Big Stone Gap Rescue Squad, Inc., is a non-profit volunteer organization dedicated to the life and property within the squad's service area and to neighboring communities when needed.

The Big Stone Gap Rescue Squad is comprised of:: 

Members

30 Full Time Members
4 Honorary Members

Location

Shawnee Avenue

 

Equipment  

3 Advanced Life Support Units
1 Heavy Duty Crash Unit
1 Light Duty Crash Unit

1997 Highlights

The squad answered 504 calls within Town limits, 406 calls in the County, for a total of 910 calls.

The squad logged 504 miles on calls in the Town limits and 8,120 miles in the County, for a total of 13,160 miles.

 

Other activities in 1997 include:

Stand-by at 4th of July Festivities
Christmas Parade, various other events
The squad hosted several EMS classes and Certified over 150 people, which includes CPR, EMT, Shock Trauma Tech, and a Cardiac Tech.
